Inside the workflow 05
You install Grammarly as a browser extension or desktop app, then it automatically appears in every text field you encounter across emails, documents, social media posts, and web forms. As you type anywhere, it underlines potential issues and shows suggestions in a sidebar or popup that you can accept or dismiss with a click.
  • —The tool pushes you toward corporate-standard writing style, potentially homogenizing your voice and discouraging creative or literary language choices
  • —Ubiquitous integration means you get the same generic suggestions everywhere rather than context-appropriate advice for different platforms or audiences
  • —Grammar checking actively flags unconventional but effective writing techniques used by literary masters, creating friction for expressive or artistic writing styles
Illustrative output 06
Prompt
Please review this email draft before I send it to my manager: 'Hi Sarah, I wanted to reach out about the project timeline. Me and the team thinks we might need additional time to complete the deliverables. The reason being is that we encountered some unexpected technical issues. Can we schedule a meeting to discuss this? Thanks, John'
Output
Grammarly would identify several issues: 1) 'Me and the team thinks' should be 'The team and I think' (subject-verb agreement and pronoun order), 2) 'The reason being is that' is wordy - suggests 'because' instead, 3) Tone detector might flag this as potentially too casual for manager communication, 4) Clarity suggestions could include being more specific about timeline extension needed. Premium features might suggest alternative phrasings like 'We've encountered unexpected technical challenges that may impact our delivery schedule' for more professional tone.
Practical interpretation
Grammarly effectively catches common grammar mistakes and provides style improvements, though users still need judgment for context-appropriate suggestions.

Overview 07

Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant that integrates across over a million different applications to provide real-time grammar, style, and writing suggestions directly at the cursor point wherever users work. The tool addresses the fundamental problem of writing quality and consistency by offering omnipresent support across web apps, desktop applications, and mobile platforms, serving 40 million daily active users who need immediate feedback without switching between different tools or platforms.

The platform operates through ubiquitous integration technology that embeds AI suggestions directly into users' existing workflows, whether they're writing emails, documents, social media posts, or academic papers. Grammarly's AI agents work contextually to provide feedback, predict reader reactions, find sources, and generate citations, while the system processes interactions across diverse writing contexts daily. The tool offers specialized features like grade predictions aligned to academic rubrics, fact-checking capabilities, and reader reaction analysis to help users revise their writing for intended impact.

Grammarly benefits most users who write frequently across multiple platforms and need consistent quality control, particularly professionals, students, and content creators who value efficiency and accessibility over writing development. However, the tool faces significant tradeoffs as critics argue it represents a 'homogenizing force' that standardizes and potentially limits writing creativity and individual voice. The company has acknowledged problematic features, with the CEO admitting that a controversial expert review feature 'wasn't good for experts' or users, leading to its removal after backlash, highlighting tensions between AI assistance and authentic writing expression.

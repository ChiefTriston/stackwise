Typical alternatives 04
ChatGPT
ChatGPT is a versatile AI tool capable of writing assistance, data analysis, and image generation, but its broad functionality comes with trade-offs compared to Grammarly's specialized focus. While ChatGPT can handle various tasks, it lacks the real-time, proactive writing refinement and seamless integration across 500,000+ apps that Grammarly provides.

Microsoft Copilot integrates into Microsoft 365 applications and excels at generating content and automating tasks within its suite, but it lacks the specialized, real-time writing refinement that Grammarly offers. Choose Microsoft Copilot when you work primarily within Microsoft 365 applications and need general productivity enhancement. Choose Grammarly when you prioritize polished communication and specialized writing assistance across diverse platforms.
Inside the workflow 05
You write in any application and Grammarly's browser extension or desktop app automatically underlines potential issues with colored indicators. You click on suggestions to accept grammar fixes, clarity improvements, or tone adjustments, then continue writing as the tool continues monitoring your text in real-time.
  • —The constant red and blue underlines can be visually distracting and interrupt your writing flow, especially during creative first drafts
  • —Premium suggestions often feel overly formal or corporate, making casual emails and social media posts sound stiff and unnatural
  • —The tool frequently flags stylistic choices as errors when they're intentional, requiring you to dismiss the same suggestions repeatedly across similar writing contexts
Illustrative output 06
Prompt
Please review this email draft before I send it to my team: 'Hi everyone, I wanted to reach out and touch base about the upcoming project deadline. We need to make sure we're all on the same page moving forward. The deliverables is due next Friday and I think we should of started preparing earlier. Let me know if you have any questions or concerns going forward.'
Output
Grammarly would identify several issues: 'deliverables is' → 'deliverables are' (subject-verb agreement), 'should of' → 'should have' (common error), and flag redundant phrases like 'reach out and touch base' and repeated 'going forward.' It would suggest alternatives like 'contact you about' and recommend removing redundancy. The clarity score might show as 'Needs work' due to wordiness, with suggestions to make the message more direct and concise.
Practical interpretation
Grammarly effectively catches common grammar mistakes and wordiness issues that could make professional communication appear less polished, though users still need to evaluate whether suggested changes match their intended tone.

Overview 07

Grammarly's AI Detector is a free tool designed to identify AI-generated text content, helping users navigate responsible AI use by providing clear scores showing what percentage of submitted text appears to be written with AI. The tool addresses the growing need for transparency and academic integrity in an era where AI writing tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude are widely accessible, allowing users to verify the authenticity of their work before submission.

The detector operates by analyzing text patterns and comparing them against known AI-generated content signatures from various AI tools including Grammarly's own AI and competitors like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude. According to Grammarly's claims, the tool achieves 99% detection accuracy and ranks #1 on RAID's independent benchmark evaluation. The system is designed to minimize false positives that wrongly flag human-written text, and it provides explanations for why specific phrases may be flagged as AI-generated, along with one-click rewrite options through their AI Rewriter feature.

The tool primarily benefits students, educators, content creators, and professionals who need to ensure their work meets authenticity requirements or verify the originality of submitted content. While Grammarly positions this as the most accurate detector available, key tradeoffs include potential dependency on their ecosystem of writing tools, the ongoing arms race between AI detection and AI generation capabilities, and the inherent limitation that no AI detector can guarantee 100% accuracy as AI writing technology continues to evolve rapidly.

Overview 07

Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant that addresses grammar, style, and writing quality issues across digital platforms. Originally launched in 2009 as a grammar checker, it has evolved into a comprehensive writing tool that now includes AI detection capabilities, claiming 99% detection accuracy and ranking #1 on RAID's independent benchmark for distinguishing human from AI-generated text. The platform integrates across over a million different apps daily, providing real-time writing assistance directly at the cursor point where users work, from email and documents to social media and web browsers.

Grammarly operates through omnipresent integration across web apps, desktop applications, and mobile platforms, serving 40 million daily active users with AI-powered suggestions for grammar, style, tone, and clarity. Its AI detector specifically identifies text generated by ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and other AI tools within seconds, while providing explanations for flagged content and offering one-click rewrites through its AI Rewriter feature. The system continuously updates to maintain detection accuracy and integrates with Grammarly's broader suite of tools including plagiarism checking and citation support, positioning itself as a comprehensive solution for responsible AI use in writing.

Grammarly benefits most writers who work across multiple digital platforms and need consistent writing quality, from students concerned about academic integrity to professionals managing high-volume communications. However, the tool faces criticism as a 'homogenizing force' that standardizes and potentially limits writing creativity and individual voice - a concern that predates its AI features. The company has acknowledged missteps, with its CEO admitting that a controversial expert review feature was removed after backlash for being ineffective for both experts and users. While Grammarly offers broad accessibility and integration, users must weigh convenience against potential over-reliance on algorithmic writing suggestions that may diminish personal writing style development.

Inside the workflow 05
You start your workday by opening Otter.ai in your browser and connecting it to your Google Calendar or Microsoft Calendar — the system automatically detects upcoming meetings. When a scheduled meeting begins, you click 'Join' from the Otter.ai dashboard, which opens your video conferencing platform (Google Meet, Teams, or Zoom) with Otter.ai recording simultaneously. The AI immediately begins transcribing speech in real-time, displaying text as people talk with automatic speaker identification showing who said what. During the 30-minute transcription session, you highlight key phrases by selecting text, add comments by clicking specific transcript sections, and assign action items directly within the conversation interface. You can pause and resume recording using dashboard controls or calendar integration buttons. The AI generates the transcript automatically, but you take control by editing speaker labels when the system misidentifies voices, correcting transcription errors in technical terms, and manually organizing the highlighted sections. After the meeting, you review the AI-generated summary, add photos for visual context through the upload feature, and share the workspace with team members who can collaborate on the same transcript.
  • —Each conversation offers 30 minutes of transcription per session, but users need to manually start new sessions for longer meetings — requiring active monitoring rather than seamless continuous recording for extended discussions
  • —Automatic speaker identification works for distinct voices, but users report needing to manually correct speaker labels when multiple people have similar vocal patterns or when someone joins mid-conversation — adding a post-meeting cleanup step
  • —Real-time transcription displays immediately during meetings, but technical jargon and industry-specific terms often appear as incorrect text that requires manual editing passes to match the actual specialized vocabulary used

Overview 07

Otter.ai is an AI-powered transcription tool that converts speech to text in real time during meetings, interviews, and lectures, with automatic speaker identification and live captions that integrate directly into video conferencing platforms.

The tool works by recording audio and generating live transcriptions with speaker identification, then uses AI to create meeting summaries tailored to specific meeting types like sales calls, team meetings, or 1:1s. It offers bot-free recording through a desktop app, integrates with business tools like Salesforce, and includes an autonomous SDR Agent that can conduct product demos for website visitors without human intervention. The platform also features AI chat functionality for interacting with meeting data and recently became HIPAA compliant for healthcare use.

Otter.ai's design clearly targets business professionals, healthcare teams, sales organizations, and content creators who need structured documentation from conversations, but some users find the AI chat feature oversells its capabilities and doesn't deliver the editing assistance it promises. The tool works best for teams that have regular meetings requiring follow-up actions and summaries, while individual users seeking simple transcription might find the business-focused features unnecessary. Journalists particularly favor it for interview transcription, though the tool's strength lies more in meeting workflows than standalone recording tasks.

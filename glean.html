Inside the workflow 05
You start your workday needing to find GitLab's policy on expense approvals. Instead of opening multiple tabs across Slack, Google Drive, the handbook, and Salesforce, you open Glean and type 'expense approval policy for travel over $500.' Glean searches across all connected systems simultaneously — GitLab handbooks, Salesforce records, Slack conversations, and Google Drive documents — but only shows you information you already have access to in each system. The AI summarizes the policy from multiple sources into a concise answer, then provides clickable links back to the original documents so you can verify details. When you need to understand why a customer deal stalled, you ask 'customer objections Q4 Enterprise deals' and Glean pulls from Gong call recordings, Salesforce opportunity notes, and Slack discussions, combining them into a single response rather than forcing you to hunt across three separate tools. You spend the rest of your session clicking through to the original sources to take action — updating the Salesforce record, following up in Slack, or scheduling the next call — because Glean shows you where information lives but doesn't replace the actual work tools.
  • —Natural language queries work well for straightforward information retrieval like 'Q4 sales targets', but GitLab users report the AI sometimes combines conflicting information from different sources without clearly flagging disagreements — requiring manual verification across multiple original documents to determine which version is current
  • —Glean connects to high-value systems like Salesforce, Gong, and Google Workspace with proper access controls intact, but enterprises switching from Glean cite 'rigid architecture' and 'long rollouts' as reasons for moving to competitors like GoSearch that offer more flexible LLM support and faster implementation
  • —The platform eliminates the multi-hour weekly searches GitLab team members previously did across fragmented tools, but it indexes content following each underlying tool's retention policies — so Gong data disappears after approximately 365 days, potentially breaking historical context in your search results

Overview 07

Glean is a company-wide AI platform that connects to all enterprise data sources to build comprehensive context across every system, learning from content, activity, and people to deliver relevant answers and automate workflows. Unlike tools embedded within specific applications, Glean operates as an ecosystem-agnostic platform that unifies information from hundreds of enterprise tools including Google Drive, Slack, Confluence, Jira, Zendesk, and Salesforce through pre-built connectors.

The platform works by maintaining permission-aware integrations that enforce access controls end-to-end while building enterprise context from connected systems. It offers native search and chat capabilities that can be embedded directly into tools like Microsoft Teams, allowing employees to ask questions and receive source-backed, AI-generated answers without switching applications. Glean also provides agents and a library of 100+ actions for running cross-stack automations that can retrieve, reason over, and execute workflows on company data across multiple platforms simultaneously.

Glean's design heavily favors organizations with complex, multi-vendor technology stacks who need to surface information scattered across numerous systems. Companies already deeply embedded in single ecosystems like Microsoft 365 may find less value, since tools like Microsoft Copilot already handle in-app assistance for those environments. The platform appears most beneficial for enterprises struggling with information silos and teams that regularly need to cross-reference data from different departments or systems, though the extensive integration setup likely requires significant IT involvement to implement effectively.

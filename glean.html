Overview 07

Glean is a company-wide AI platform that solves the universal workplace problem of finding information and people within enterprise organizations. Unlike application-specific AI assistants, Glean connects to all enterprise data sources—including Google Workspace, Slack, Confluence, Jira, Zendesk, Salesforce, Microsoft tools, and hundreds of other systems—to provide a unified search and knowledge discovery experience. The platform addresses the challenge that every knowledge worker faces: locating relevant information scattered across multiple systems and silos within their company.

Glean operates as a conversational AI assistant similar to ChatGPT but with full company context, connecting to enterprise systems initially in read-only mode to build trust before adding advanced capabilities. The platform integrates through 100+ turnkey, permission-aware connectors that maintain existing access controls end-to-end, ensuring users only see information they're authorized to access. It learns from content, activity, and people patterns to deliver relevant, source-backed answers and can automate workflows across connected systems. The platform functions ecosystem-agnostically, working within tools like Microsoft Teams while maintaining connections to non-Microsoft systems, contrasting with ecosystem-bound solutions like Microsoft Copilot that primarily work within specific application suites.

Glean benefits large enterprises most, with deployments at major organizations including T-Mobile, Uber, and Dell, demonstrating its ability to work within challenging legacy environments and conservative risk profiles. The platform is particularly valuable for knowledge workers who regularly need to find information across multiple systems and for organizations seeking to break down information silos. Key tradeoffs include the complexity of enterprise-wide integration versus the comprehensive context it provides, and the initial setup investment required to connect multiple data sources versus the long-term productivity gains from unified information access.
